What about Josh Bliss?

Joshua is a student at Western Sydney University, studying diploma of ICT with the goal to finish his bachelors in Cyber Security. Outside of school, although sharing the same interests with what he's learning acedemically, his hobbies consists of gaming, soccer, and tinkering linux systems. He follows the philosophies of free and open source (FOSS) and minimalism. It's one of the many anchors that guide him through today's society.

Technologies

In this image, it shows the various technologies I use. I have a NASA desktop PC that I personally built, primarily for gaming - It is dual booting Windows (Only for gaming. I also hacked it so there is no bloat, ads, or Microsoft spyware - even fully uninstalled the Microsoft Edge Browser.) and Omarchy (an arch based distro for studying, tinkering, and general use). The laptops I have are the 2015 Macbook air and a 2011 Thinkpad X220. I was handed down the Macbook air for free and hacked linux onto it so it can perform better in 2025 for my studies. Planning for my bachelors course, I needed a better laptop for doing Cyber Security. So I got myself a Thinkpad X220, although older than the Macbook, the hardware is upgradable and can out perform the Macbook air in 2026-2028. Thinkpads are good for leatning about the hardware as well as the software. Finally I have a Raspberry Pi 5. Which is Primarily for home labbing - experimenting with: self hosting, VPN, network filtering/ firewall configuring, environment to learn hacking. It's a small piece of hardware yet it's more powerfull than both of my laptops.
